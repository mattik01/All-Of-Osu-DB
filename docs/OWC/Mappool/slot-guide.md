# OWC mappool — slot guide

Per-slot descriptions of what each mappool slot tests, drawn primarily from the 2022 reddit "OWC Mappool Viewer's Guide" (Cytusine et al.) with notes on how each slot has evolved across editions. For pool sizes and round structure, see [`pool-structure.md`](./pool-structure.md). Mod definitions (HD, HR, DT, FM, TB rules): [`../../knowledge-base/game/mods.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/mods.md). Difficulty attributes (AR, CS, OD, SR): [`../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md).

The skill labels below ("aim consistency", "streams", "tech", etc.) are **selector conventions, not formal rules**. Slot identities are most strict in the 2019–2021 era and most permissive in 2022+ after the announced rebalance toward less-isolated skillsets. The scraper records the slot label verbatim from Liquipedia; the *meaning* below is interpretive and may not match every individual map in every edition.

## Slot quick-reference

| Slot | Skillset (2019–2021 reference frame) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `NM1` | Aim consistency | longest map; "generic" jump aim |
| `NM2` | Streams | 1/4 stream stamina or spaced flow streams |
| `NM3` | Alternating | low-BPM dense alt patterns |
| `NM4` | Tech | unconventional sliders, SV changes |
| `NM5` | Speed (until 2021) / "miscellaneous" (2022+) | speed slot was moved to FM2 in 2022 |
| `NM6` | Miscellaneous (until 2021) — slot does not exist 2022+ | renamed `NM5` in 2022 |
| `HD1` | Reading consistency | dense / awkward aim under HD |
| `HD2` | Low-AR reading | sub-9 AR, reading-heavy |
| `HD3` | Miscellaneous | fills HD pool gaps |
| `HR1` | Aim consistency under HR | smaller circles, tighter OD |
| `HR2` | Precision aim | very small CS (≥6.5 effective) |
| `HR3` | Miscellaneous | typically tech or low-BPM rhythm under HR |
| `DT1` | Aim consistency under DT | high AR, J-pop / anime songs |
| `DT2` | Stamina speed | sustained 1/4 streams at 220–240 BPM |
| `DT3` | Fast speed | 250–270 BPM bursts |
| `DT4` | Miscellaneous | finger control / alt / high-AR reaction |
| `FM1` | Aim-focused (HD/HR-tolerant) | low AR (~9) for HD-friendliness |
| `FM2` | Antimod (until 2021) / Speed (2022+) | speed moved here in 2022; pre-2022 was the "low AR + small CS" antimod slot |
| `FM3` | Miscellaneous (until 2021) / Antimod (2022+) | renamed from `FM2` in 2022 |
| `FM4` | (does not exist pre-2022) / Miscellaneous (2022+) | renamed from `FM3` in 2022 |
| `TB` | Balanced marathon | 5+ minute, all-skills, mods optional per player |

## No Mod (NM)

Largest bracket. No mods forced; teams pick which 4 of 8 players play.

### NM1 — Aim Consistency

Comfortable jump patterns at moderate spacing (1–2 jump distances, star patterns, predictable angles). Maps tend to be **long** (3:30+) and "generic" — J-pop, J-rock, anime openings are common. Few long bursts; this slot is about sustained aim rather than tapping. Pool SR is generally dictated by `NM1`.

Reddit-guide reference examples:
- Jin ft. MARiA from GARNiDELiA — daze [captin's Extra]
- Roselia — R [Rosenlied]
- Aqours — Kimeta yo Hand in Hand [Unity]

Evolution: stable across all editions from 2015+. Pre-bracket editions (2011–2012) had no slot identity here; the `NM1` slot was just the first numbered map.

### NM2 — Streams

Series of consecutive 1/4 notes. Tests **flow aim** (spaced streams) and/or **stream stamina** (very long streams). BPM 170–190 for 5–6★ pools, 190–210 for harder pools. Electronic / metal songs with drum or synth backing.

Examples:
- technoplanet — Juvenile [Extra]
- ZUN remixed by LeaF — Wizdomiot [Rational Punishment]
- Xi — Densetsu no Sabori Shinigami ~ Make a quick escape [Extra Stage]

### NM3 — Alternating

"Alt" maps: lower BPM (130–160) but **densely** mapped, with 1/4 patterns interspersed with sliders for rhythm complexity, and a mix of flow + snap aim. Requires active pattern processing.

Examples:
- POLKADOT STINGRAY — SHAKE! SHAKE! (Shizuku Kariuta ver.) [MOPH'S EXPERT!]
- Kaf — Montage [pkk's Extra]
- KINEMA106 — N.M.W [NO MORE WAR]

### NM4 — Tech

Tech maps: unconventional slider shapes, drastically varying slider velocity, control elements (doubles, cutstreams, occasional rhythm changes). Songs tend to be electronic or instrumental with heavy "wubs" from synthesizers.

Examples:
- tokiwa — Syuou Sange [Mir's Expert]
- seatrus — MONONOKE [Orientalism]
- Feryquitous — Hemizorv [Dissonance]

### NM5 — Speed (pre-2022) / Miscellaneous (2022+)

**Pre-2022** (2019–2021 reference): high-BPM speed (220+ BPM). Common patterns: short bursts with mild finger control (breakcore-style), or stamina speed (sustained bursts).

**2022+**: the speed slot was moved to `FM2`. The current `NM5` slot is the **former `NM6`** — a "miscellaneous" slot covering whatever the rest of the NM pool doesn't (rhythm control, low AR, stacks/overlaps, etc.).

Pre-2022 examples (when this was the speed slot):
- Thousand Leaves — Temptation [Extra]
- Laur — Nostalgic Blood of the Strife [MAXIMUM]
- goreshit — xenobeat [DeathleSS]

### NM6 — Miscellaneous (pre-2022 only)

**Pre-2022 only.** Filled gaps: rhythm control (1/3 ↔ 1/4 switches), reading-heavy patterns, low AR, stacks/overlaps. In 2022 this slot was renamed to `NM5` (since the old speed `NM5` moved to FM).

Pre-2022 examples:
- Frums — 24eeev0-$
- xi — Mirage Garden [Down's Extra]
- Pastel*Palettes — Happy Synthesizer [Special]

## Hidden (HD)

HD: notes fade after appearing. Tests **reading** and how the player handles reading combined with other skills.

### HD1 — Reading Consistency

The "main" HD pick. May test any of: dense aim at high BPM, awkward aim (wide angles, linear/geometric patterns), stacks and overlaps, fast sliders, or rhythmically complex patterns. The defining trait is that all of these are made harder by the HD fade.

Examples:
- BLOOD CODE — POP SHOVE-IT FEAT. TOFUKU [Combo Breaker]
- KikuoHana — Desert Theater [Aistre's Extra]
- Yuma Mizonokuchi feat. Ai Ohsera — Tie me down gently -long ver- [Ultra]

### HD2 — Low-AR Reading

**Lower AR than the rest of the pool** (often AR 8 or below). Reading-heavy: stacks, unintuitive spacing, awkward aim, sometimes unconventional rhythm. SR tends to be lower than other pool maps because the star-rating algorithm does not capture reading difficulty proportional to its actual challenge.

Examples:
- x0o0x_ — Big Brother [DADA'S INSANE Mix]
- Porno Graffitti — Hitori no Yoru [Insane]
- Chikamori Kayako — Kigurumi Sungeki [Labyrinth of Steam]

### HD3 — Miscellaneous

Fills HD pool gaps and balances BPM / aim style across the bracket. Common picks: low-BPM flow aim, swing rhythm, alt or tech maps similar to `NM3` / `NM4`. Streams are common here if not already represented in `HD1` / `HD2`.

Examples:
- Michiru Kagemori (CV: Morohoshi Sumire) — Ready to (Mokelmbe Bootleg) [Furry Bootleg]
- xi — .357 Magnum [High Noon]
- Nishigomi Kakumi — Gekka Bijin [Oni (OWC Edit Ver.)]

## Hard Rock (HR)

HR: smaller circles (CS ×1.4), higher AR, stricter OD, vertically flipped playfield. Tests skills affected by these stat changes.

### HR1 — Aim Consistency

Like `NM1` but with smaller circles and tighter timing. Often a different BPM or aim style than the corresponding `NM1` for variety within the pool.

Examples:
- Kino Hiko — The Noise of Rain [Extra]
- AliA — impulse [Akitoshi's Extreme]
- Wavves — Super Soaker [Stupid]

### HR2 — Precision Aim

**Very small circles**, usually CS 6.5+ effective after HR is applied. Tests patterns that get harder with small notes: snappy aim, spaced bursts, slider jumps. Streams and flow aim may also feature because of the tighter accuracy requirement.

Examples:
- Yooh — Dynasty [HalLoWeeN's EXHAUST]
- Brandy — Cross Time !! [captin's Extra]
- WHITEFISTS — VECTOR [Beyond]

### HR3 — Miscellaneous

Fills HR pool gaps. **Tech** is a common pick here (HR + tech is uniquely punishing). Low-BPM flow / rhythm-control maps also appear since HR makes accurate hits on awkward rhythms much harder.

Examples:
- ak+q — Vexaria [Insane]
- Polyphia — O.D. [Another]
- Hana — crocodile tears [Facade]

## Double Time (DT)

DT: 1.5× speed, ~×1.12 effective AR / CS. Tests skills that involve fast movement and reaction.

### DT1 — Aim Consistency

High AR (8.8–9.2 before DT, ≈10.2–10.5 after). Higher BPM than other consistency slots — typically 220–250 BPM after DT applied. Comfortable snap aim and burst patterns; reaction is the differentiator. Often J-pop / anime songs.

Examples:
- WEAVER — Kuchizuke Diamond [Shizuku's Light Insane]
- Inui Toko — Kimagure Romantic [Amateurre & sanairrt's Insane]
- Yukinoshita Yukino (CV. Hayami Saori) & Yuigahama Yui (CV. Touyama Nao) — Everyday World [Kibbleru's Insane]

### DT2 — Stamina Speed

**Sustained** long bursts / streams at 220–240 BPM after DT. Aim is de-emphasised; the test is maintaining stream stamina with mechanical difficulty.

Examples:
- Analog digitaL — IBUKI [Insane]
- Lime — Noblesse Gloria [Noblesse]
- Nightwish — Sacrament of Wilderness [Insane (OWC)]

### DT3 — Fast Speed

5–9 note **bursts** at 250–270 BPM after DT. Less rhythmically complex than `NM5` (pre-2022 speed slot) or `DT2`, but requires fast tapping mixed with aim.

Examples:
- Yuyoyuppe — AiAe [Insane]
- Hatsuki Yura — Junketsu to Toki no Tenbin [Insane OTST]
- EPICA — Fight Your Demons [fight your primal urges]

### DT4 — Miscellaneous

Complements the rest of the DT bracket. Common: **finger control** (doubles, tech-tapping), high-BPM "alternating" DT (280+ BPM after DT, flow patterns), or high-AR reaction (tech elements). Often incorporates aim control (irregular angles, irregular spacing).

Examples:
- mafumafu — I wanna be a girl [TheBlank's Insane]
- Hatsuki Yura — Koisuru Marionette [Insane]
- Akatsuki Records — Adieu, to this Lively Graveyard [KIRBY REMIX DELUXE EDITION]

## Free Mod (FM)

Each team must field at least 1 HD player and 1 HR (or HDHR) player; remaining 2 players choose freely. Teams typically run 2 NM + 1 HD + 1 HR.

### FM1 — Aim-Focused

Comfortable on whichever mod a player picks. Often **lower AR** (~AR 9) to increase note density and make HD-reading more challenging. Flow aim or awkward aim patterns similar to `HD1`.

Examples:
- FujuniseikouyuuP — FREEDMAN [Nouk]
- Akatsuki Records — Trance Dance Anarchy [Dancing!]
- Falcom Sound Team jdk — Infinity Rage [Stage 4]

### FM2 — Speed (2022+) / Antimod (pre-2022)

**Pre-2022**: the **antimod** slot — low AR (≈8) and small CS (≈5). Linear "old-mapping-style" patterns. The slot's name comes from being uniquely punishing on either HD (more rhythm processing under low AR) or HR (more precision on already small circles).

**2022+**: the speed slot moved here from `NM5`. The pre-2022 antimod role moved to `FM3`.

Pre-2022 antimod examples:
- Hatsune Miku — Hidari Migi -migihidari- [Rin]
- SHK — Identity Part III [SHD]
- connsome — flat by second [Another]

### FM3 — Antimod (2022+) / Miscellaneous (pre-2022)

**Pre-2022**: the miscellaneous FM slot — low BPM flow aim, swing, alt, tech, often **large circles** (CS < 4) so HR's effect is reduced.

**2022+**: this is the **former `FM2` antimod** slot.

Examples (these are the pre-2022 miscellaneous picks):
- ayaponzu* — Justice Monster [Extra]
- nobodyknows+ — Kokoro Odoru [bbj's Expert]
- Toby "Radiation" Fox — Black [Make Her a Member of the OWC Crew]

### FM4 — Miscellaneous (2022+ only)

**Does not exist pre-2022.** Created by the `NM5 → FM2` rebalance: the former `FM3` miscellaneous slot was renamed to `FM4`.

## Tiebreaker (TB)

A single map per stage, played only if the score is tied at the penultimate point. **Marathon length** (5+ minutes), tests a variety of skillsets, often the most difficult map in the pool. **Mods are optional per player**.

In several editions the Grand Finals tiebreaker is a **commissioned original** — see [`pool-structure.md`](./pool-structure.md#tiebreaker-tradition).

Examples:
- Dance Gavin Dance — NASA / I'm Down with Brown Town [Reminiscence]
- DJ SHARPNEL — FAKE PROMISE [NISEMONO]
- Camellia — OOPARTS [Zenith] (OWC 2020 GF)
- Yooh — RPG [Divinity] (OWC 2021 GF)
- Camellia — Operation: Zenithfall (OWC 2024 F + GF)

## Glossary

Selector / commentator terminology used across the slots above. Where a term has a canonical definition outside OWC, this glossary cross-links rather than redefining.

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **alt** | Alternating: 1/4 (or finer) tap patterns at moderate BPM (130–170), played by alternating fingers rather than single-tapping. Distinct from "stream" (which is also 1/4 but typically continuous and at higher BPM). |
| **antimod** | A pool slot deliberately chosen so that **either** HD **or** HR makes it disproportionately harder. Typical recipe: low AR + small CS + linear aim patterns. Pre-2022 lived at `FM2`; the term comes from "playing it without a mod is the easiest option, but FM rules force one player to take HD or HR". |
| **AR change** | A map whose AR differs from the pool's typical AR; usually low-AR (e.g., `HD2`, `FM1`, `FM2` antimod), sometimes high-AR (e.g., `DT1`). |
| **burst** | A short run of consecutive notes (typically 3–9), distinct from a "stream" (sustained run). |
| **CS** (Circle Size) | Hit-circle radius. Smaller CS = harder. See [`difficulty-attributes.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md). |
| **finger control** | Patterns that demand precise fingering — repeated doubles, varying note spacing in time, kick-sliders, tech rhythms. Common in `DT4`. |
| **flow aim** | Aim where consecutive note positions follow a smooth curve; "snap aim" is the contrast (sharp angle changes between notes). |
| **gimmick** | A map with mechanic relying on a specific reading or visual trick (e.g., off-beat sliders, invisible objects, visual misdirection). Rare in OWC pools. |
| **HDHR** | Hidden + Hard Rock combined. The hardest valid `FM` choice. |
| **jump map** | A map dominated by large spaced single-notes; the prototypical `NM1` / `HR1` / `DT1` style. |
| **low-AR** | AR ≤ 8 (with the modifier used). Reading-heavy. |
| **OD** (Overall Difficulty) | Hit-timing window strictness. Higher OD = stricter timing. See [`difficulty-attributes.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md). |
| **precision** | Aim under small CS where the difficulty is hitting *into* the circle, not getting *to* it in time. `HR2` slot. |
| **reading** | The act of decoding what to play before playing it. Made harder by HD (fade), low AR (fast appearance), stacks/overlaps, or unintuitive spacing. |
| **rhythm control** | Maps that switch between rhythm divisors (e.g., 1/3 ↔ 1/4) or use unusual time signatures. |
| **snap aim** | Contrast to flow aim — sharp directional changes, often "X-pattern" or "back-and-forth" jumps. |
| **stamina speed** | Sustained streams or bursts that test the player's ability to keep tapping accurately for an extended duration. `DT2` slot. |
| **stream** | A continuous run of 1/4 notes (typically at 170–210 BPM), tapped with alternating fingers or single-handed. |
| **SV** (Slider Velocity) | The speed at which sliders travel. Tech slots (`NM4`, `HR3`) often vary SV drastically. |
| **swing** | A rhythm style where 1/4 patterns are played with uneven feel (e.g., 2:1 within a beat). Specific to certain musical genres (jazz, some breakcore). |
| **tech** | Maps with unconventional slider shapes, varying SV, cutstreams, doubles, rhythm switches. `NM4` is the canonical slot. |

For canonical definitions of mods (HD, HR, DT, FM rules, NF, SD), see [`../../knowledge-base/game/mods.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/mods.md). For AR / CS / OD / SR formulas and ranges, see [`../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md`](../../knowledge-base/game/difficulty-attributes.md).
